Web Site Critiques

Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Trader Joe’s – http://www.traderjoes.com/

  1. Bright, seasonal, and feels on-brand and welcoming.
  2. Top header with key links (Stores, About, Careers, Announcements, Search, Shopping List) and a large hero promoting the “Fearless Flyer”; a vertical ribbon on the left groups sections like Products, Discover, Recipes, and Listen.
  3. The structure makes sense: clear global nav at the top, section shortcuts on the left, and content guiding you to products. It seems like there is a lot going on which could confuse some people.
  4. High-quality photography and clean card layouts enhance the experience. They are seasonal pictures.
  5. It loaded quickly for me; very reasonable for a homepage.
  6. This was readible for me. Light colored background with black lettering on top of it.
  7. Yes, both a top navigation and a left “ribbon” tool are easy to use and consistent.
  8. Warm reds/earth tones and textured neutrals are repeated throughout which give it an inviting look and feel.
  9. Responsive elements reflow into stacked sections. It requires more searching and clicking to find what you need, but I feel it is created well.
  10. When you scroll down there are seasonal products that they are selling. They included recipes that you can use that product for. I feel this is unusual and very well done for a grocery store.

Pitchfork – http://pitchfork.com/

  1. Minimalist, editorial vibe with a bold black masthead—clearly a media site.
  2. Horizontal category navigation (News, Reviews, Best New Music, Features, Lists, Columns, Video) with a content grid and a “Latest” sidebar located on the right.
  3. Structure is logical for a magazine: categories up top, featured stories large, and timely items ordered on the right in a feed.
  4. Strong typography and large imagery create a magazine-style layout and ad slots are visible but don’t ruin the design.
  5. The load time was reasonable.
  6. High contrast black/white palette with multiple font sizes. The only issue is that it is all black and white, which makes a lot of the coloring blurr together.
  7. Yes, a clear global navigation and section filters; the navigation feels standard and effective for browsing content.
  8. Consistent theme with accent images; the look feels uniform across sections.
  9. The grid collapses into stacked articles on smaller screens. It is not my favorite, but it is not terrible.
  10. Signature “Reviews” and “Best New Music” labeling provide quick value for music discovery.

Jamilin – http://www.jamilin.com/

  1. Busy and old first impression with many colors, badges, and large banners.
  2. Primary navigation appears as colorful tab-like buttons; content sections (e.g., Galactic Spirit, Reverse Aging, Feng Shui) are presented as distinct tiles and links.
  3. Somewhat confusing: many topics compete for attention and everything isn’t consistent, so it takes effort to decide where to click.
  4. Graphics look low-resolution; the crowded layout detracts from usability and makes scanning harder.
  5. The load time was acceptable.
  6. Readability varies: some headings have adequate contrast, but several color combinations (bright text on colored backgrounds) make it harder to read.
  7. There is a navigation area, but the design and labeling are inconsistent, which makes it less effective.
  8. Color usage is not cohesive; different bright palettes across sections reduce the sense of a unified look and feel.
  9. Appears minimally responsive; elements don’t reflow cleanly and can require pinching/zooming on small screens.
  10. The eclectic mix of topics (Feng Shui, skincare, “Galactic Spirit”) is unusual and may appeal to a very specific audience.
